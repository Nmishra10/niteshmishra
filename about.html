Have you ever had problems putting the wide range of topics of social psychology together to establish a unified field? Instead of presenting a cohesive set of concepts and expertise, the author felt like he was giving a laundry list of ideas, research findings, and phenomena. He wondered how his students would retain and coprehend the wide range of phenomena studied by social psychologist. what were they to do if they couldn't establish what was most important? The new era of psychology began when it was realised that a test approach to a social psychology text books was required to structure and integrate student learning.
